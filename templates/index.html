Workstream RACI

Cross-functional ownership at the workstream level. Boundary tasks between workstreams are the highest drop risk — explicit RACI prevents them.

RACI-Lite Matrix — Key Decisions & Handoffs

A Accountable — single owner who answers for the outcome. There can only be one A per activity. If two teams share A, no one is truly accountable.
R Responsible — executes the work. Multiple R entries are allowed. The A may also be R, but R alone does not confer ownership.
C Consulted — must be asked before the decision is finalised. Their input is required; they have a voice before the decision locks.
I Informed — notified after the decision is made. No input required; they need visibility to coordinate downstream work.
⚡

Boundary Task Risk

Tasks that live at the edge between two workstreams are the most common source of dropped balls. When Data hands off to Engineering, or Engineering hands off to Launch — neither team naturally owns that interface. A RACI-lite model makes it explicit: one person is Accountable per task, even at handoffs. Log every cross-workstream dependency in the RAID register with an owner and resolution SLA.
